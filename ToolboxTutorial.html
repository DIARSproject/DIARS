Welcome to the DIARS toolbox

This website is part of the DIARS project that bring together European experts from remote sensing and ecology to demonstrate and characterize the impact of invasive species on ecosystems through the combined use of field data and data obtained through remote-sensing technologies and support the monitoring and prediction of spread and risk assessment of invasive plant species.

The toolbox contains tutorials that walk you through a remote sensing-based framework for mapping, monitoring and assessing the impact of biological invasions. The toolbox has been divided in two sections (data preparation and applications) and seven subsections (hyperspectral, LiDAR data processing, field sampling, mapping, monitoring and impact assessment). All sections have been developed in Free and Open Source Software.

Before starting…

to work with this tutorial, make sure you have a recent version of R and GRASS GIS installed and ready to use in your computer.

Click on the section you are interested in to have access to the tutorial. In each section you will find the aim, software required and a link to download the dataset used. Keep in mind that the outcomes obtained from the data preparation section might be required for some of the applications.

Data preparation

Hyperspectral and LiDAR data contain complex information on the chemistry and 3D structure of the forest. Thus, to accurately derive meaningful variables for biological invasions it is necessary to perform some data pre-processing.

This remote sensing data can help improve the field sampling design. Here we also include a method to generate a field samples that include the environmental heterogeneity.

Applications

The variables derived from the remote sensing data are used to detect and map invasive species distribution (Mapping), to predict the spread of the invasive species and identify vulnerable areas via species distribution models (Ecosystem monitoring), and to assess their impact on the ecosystem (Impact assessment).
